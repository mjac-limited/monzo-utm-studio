UTM Parameter Reference
Quick reference for all UTM parameters used in Monzo's GA4 tracking setup.
What are UTM tags and why do we use them?
What is a UTM tag?

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are short snippets of text added to the end of a URL. When someone clicks a tagged link, Google Analytics 4 reads those parameters and records exactly where that visitor came from, what channel they used, and which campaign or piece of content drove them there.

Why is it important for Monzo?

Without UTM tags, GA4 labels most traffic as "direct" or "unassigned" — making it impossible to tell whether a sign-up came from a partner article, a BD email, or a social post. UTM tags give every link a clear identity, so the team can see exactly which channels and campaigns are driving growth.

Filtering campaign reports in GA4

In GA4, head to Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition and filter by utm_campaign or utm_medium to see sessions, conversions, and revenue attributed to a specific campaign. Consistent naming across all links is what makes this filtering reliable post-campaign.

Always-on best practice

Tag every outbound link that lands on a Monzo property — whether it's a partner article, a BD email, a social post, or a QR code at an event. Untagged links quietly pollute your analytics. Consistent UTM hygiene means the data you see in GA4 after a campaign is trustworthy and actionable.

utm_source
Identifies where the traffic came from — the referring website, platform, or tool. For referral links use the partner domain; for social use the platform name.

utm_medium
The channel type. GA4 uses this for its default channel grouping, so values should follow the channel taxonomy: referral, email, outreach, event, social, cpc.

utm_campaign
Groups all links belonging to one marketing initiative. Use underscores, keep it lowercase. This is the primary dimension for campaign-level analysis in GA4.

utm_content
Distinguishes multiple links within the same campaign — e.g. different CTAs in the same email, or different placements on the same page. Always add this when there are multiple links.
